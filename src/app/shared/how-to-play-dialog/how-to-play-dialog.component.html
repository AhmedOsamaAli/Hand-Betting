The twist — tile drift

Number tiles never change. But every Wind and Dragon tile in your new hand shifts after each round:

Win +1 to every Wind / Dragon in your next hand
Loss −1 to every Wind / Dragon in your next hand

The new value sticks to that specific tile. When it goes back to the deck, it remembers — so the same East Wind can come back later as a 7, or a 3, or a 9.

⚠ If any tile's value reaches {{ tileMin }} or {{ tileMax }}, it breaks and the game ends immediately.

The deck — Draw, Discard & Reshuffle

The whole game runs on 136 Mahjong tiles split into two piles. Three numbers in the top-right tell you where every tile is right now:

  • Draw — tiles still face-down, waiting to be dealt. Starts at 136 and drops by {{ handSize }} every round.
  • Discard — tiles you've already seen. They're not gone — they sit here until the next reshuffle puts them back in play.
  • Reshuffle — counter, e.g. 1 / {{ maxReshuffles }}. When the draw pile can't deal {{ handSize }} more tiles, the discard pile is shuffled back in automatically and this counter ticks up.

You never trigger a shuffle yourself — it just happens when the deck runs low. After {{ maxReshuffles }} reshuffles, the game ends on the next empty deck, even if every tile is still healthy.
